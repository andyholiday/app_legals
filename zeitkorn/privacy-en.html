2. What data is processed?

2.1 Account data

For optional cloud synchronisation (Supabase), the following data may be collected:

  • Email address (for email-based registration)
  • Authentication token (session token, stored in the device Keychain)
  • Apple ID identifier (for Sign in with Apple, once enabled)

All app content can also be used entirely locally, without an account.

2.2 Content data (user-generated)

This data is entered solely by you and stored locally (optionally synchronised via cloud):

  • Child's name
  • Date of birth and optionally place of birth and birth measurements (height, weight)
  • Optional gender
  • Milestones: date, category, title, note, sticker identifier
  • Photos attached to milestones (EXIF-stripped before storage, see Section 5)

2.3 Consent records

The app records your consent decisions (e.g. for future analytics) with a timestamp, type, and status — stored locally and optionally on the server.

2.4 Technical operational data

Internally used, non-personal operational data:

  • Synchronisation queue (local queue, never transmitted directly)
  • App metadata (e.g. timestamp of last sync, anonymous device identifier)
  • Preferences (language, colour scheme, display mode) — UI preferences only, no personal reference

3. Purposes and legal bases of processing

3.1 App operation and milestone documentation

Purpose: Storage and display of content data entered by you (milestones, photos, baby data).

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (performance of a contract / pre-contractual measures) — the processing is necessary to fulfil the usage contract (app purchase).

3.2 Cloud synchronisation (optional)

Purpose: Cross-device synchronisation of your data via Supabase (EU server, Frankfurt).

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR (performance of a contract) — you consciously activate this feature.

3.3 Anonymous usage analytics (PostHog – not yet active)

Purpose: Improving app quality through anonymised usage events.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR (consent) — PostHog will only be initialised after explicit consent. Baby data (name, date of birth, photos, milestone notes) will never be transmitted to PostHog.

Current status: PostHog is not active in the current app version. The library is present, but initialisation is implemented as an explicitly disabled stub in the code (GDPR Art. 9, zero telemetry before consent). A future version may send anonymised events, provided you give consent.

3.4 Crash reporting (Sentry – not yet active)

Purpose: Error detection and fixing to improve app stability.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR (consent) — also not yet active, same consent-gate logic as PostHog.

4. Storage location – EU region

Your cloud data stays in the EU. If you use cloud synchronisation, your data is stored on Supabase servers in the EU region (Frankfurt, Germany). No transfer to third countries outside the EEA takes place without appropriate safeguards.

Photos are stored locally on your device and — if cloud sync is enabled — uploaded EXIF-stripped to the Supabase Storage bucket of the same EU project.

5. EXIF stripping for photos

Before a photo is saved or uploaded, the app removes all EXIF metadata from the image file. This includes in particular GPS coordinates, device information, and capture timestamps. Stripping occurs on your device before any bytes leave it.

The photo is scaled to a maximum edge length of 2,048 pixels (aspect ratio preserved) and saved at 90 % JPEG quality. A thumbnail version (max. 320 pixels) is also created locally.

6. Retention periods and deletion

  • Local data: Remains on your device until you use the "Delete account" feature or uninstall the app.
  • Cloud data (Supabase): Fully deleted when you execute "Delete account" (GDPR Art. 17). Soft-deleted milestones are permanently purged from the system by a nightly database job.
  • Photos: Deleted from both the device and Supabase Storage when the account is deleted.
  • Authentication tokens: Deleted from the device Keychain when the account is deleted.

7. Your rights as a data subject

Under the GDPR you have the following rights:

  • Access (Art. 15): You can request information about your stored data at any time.
  • Rectification (Art. 16): You can correct inaccurate data directly in the app.
  • Erasure / Right to be forgotten (Art. 17): Under Settings → "Delete account", all personal data is completely deleted locally and on the server.
  • Data portability / Export (Art. 20): Under Settings → "Export data", you can download all your data as a ZIP archive (manifest.json + photos) for reuse.
  • Object (Art. 21): Where data is processed on the basis of legitimate interests, you may object.
  • Withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)): Consent given (e.g. for analytics) can be withdrawn at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (Art. 77): You have the right to complain to a data protection supervisory authority. In Germany, this is, for example, the competent state authority or the Federal Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information (BfDI).

To exercise your rights (to the extent not directly possible in the app), please contact: an.creat1985@gmail.com.

11. Analytics minimisation

PostHog is not active in the current app version. While the source code includes the PostHog library, the initialisation is an explicit no-op stub (per GDPR Art. 9: zero telemetry before consent).

Should a future version activate analytics, the following applies:

  • Only anonymised events (e.g. "milestone captured", "export started") without user-generated content.
  • No baby names, dates of birth, photos, notes, or other content-related data sent to PostHog.
  • Activation only after explicit in-app consent.
  • PostHog EU hosting (Frankfurt) — no transfer outside the EU.

12. Deletion and export functions in the app

12.1 Delete account (GDPR Art. 17)

Under Settings → "Delete account", the following data is deleted:

  • All local database rows: babies, milestones, photos, consent records, synchronisation queue, app metadata
  • All photo and thumbnail files on the device
  • Session tokens in the Keychain
  • Supabase session (sign-out from the server)

Milestone templates are excluded from deletion (non-personal catalogue; reseeded on the next app start).

12.2 Data export (GDPR Art. 20)

Under Settings → "Export data", a ZIP archive is created containing:

  • manifest.json: all baby data, milestones, photo metadata, consent records in JSON format
  • photos/: all locally present photos (EXIF-stripped)

The archive contains no absolute device paths and no internal system data.
